### Member rationales (≤ 200 tok each)

- **UP01 End-User**: "C wins because I already know the gesture grammar — full-bleed 9:16, right-rail actions. The 'why' chip in-frame answers the question I'd ask out loud. B reads like a magazine I'd subscribe to. D feels respectful of my attention. A is gorgeous but I'd photograph it, not use it. E is fun but I want content first, points second."
- **UP02 Designer**: "A is the only mockup whose first frame is unmistakably *designed*. 132pt mixed-blend headline + halftone numeral '09' + rouge/pop/teal triad = editorial confidence. B is its restrained sibling. D is brave counter-aesthetic. C is competent but conventional dark-mode app. E reads as 'app skin' rather than design system."
- **UP03 Accessibility**: "B passes WCAG 2.2 AA most cleanly: high contrast paper/ink, real text not text-in-image, semantic article elements. D similar — readable serif, clear hierarchy. C uses backdrop-filter chips with low contrast on video — flag. A's 132pt + mixed-blend-mode worries me on low-vision. E aurora gradients + bright pink on purple risk fail."
- **UP04 IA**: "B has the cleanest mental model: today's lede → 9-of-412 dossier → cards. D mirrors with publication metaphor. A buries the 9 picks below a hero. C is single-card focus, weak for browse. E layers 4 navigations (XP/streak/quests/badges) before content — IA debt."
- **UP05 Mobile UX**: "C is the only one designed *for the device 87% of the audience uses*. Phone bezel mockup, thumb-zone right rail, install-toast = all correct. A and B are desktop-first and would need significant rework. E gamification requires mobile but the layout shown is desktop. D respects mobile via narrow column."
- **UP06 Content Designer**: "D's blurbs are extraordinary — 'A 38-second portrait that the YouTube algorithm would never have shown you. Filed under: hands at work.' That sentence is the entire pitch. B's voice is editor-warm. A's headline 'Out of *your* bubble' is the demo opener. C's copy is functional. E's quest names ('Cartographer', 'Diversifier') are clever but utility-first."
- **UP07 Usability Tester**: "Heuristic eval — C: clearest task path (tap → watch → why). E: most engaging on first-session metric (4 quests = 4 hooks). B: best for browse task. A: best for share-as-image. D: best for read task. C+E share the strongest *return* incentive."
- **UP08 Brand Voice**: "D *is* the brand voice — 'No infinite feed, no autoplay, no agent in the room' — that's a positioning statement, not a tagline. B's 'reads taste like a magazine editor reads readers' is brand poetry. A's 'Bubble jailbreak ed.' stamp is brand swagger. C is voiceless. E borrows Duolingo's voice."
- **UP09 Visual Designer**: "A: type, color, halftone, mixed-blend — every craft variable used deliberately. B: editorial restraint mastered. D: print revivalism done with intention. E: rich palette but generic dashboard composition. C: clean but I've seen this dark-mode video player 100 times."
- **UP10 Motion Designer**: "A's halftone numeral begs to *number-up* in the demo (412 → 09). E has obvious motion entry points (XP fill, streak flame, quest tick). C has video-loop autoplay. B and D are static-by-design. For a 2-min demo where the first 30s must move, A and E win on motion potential."

## 3. UP Verdict — Ranked Top 3

### 🥇 #1 · the-design-forward (38 pts)
**Why it wins UX**: Best-in-class evocation of 발견의 즐거움 through *editorial restraint* — paper-tone canvas, hand-annotation marginalia, "joy of discovery" surfaced in plain English in the masthead. Strongest IA (lede → 9-of-412 → cards), high accessibility, brand voice that reads like a magazine reader's rather than an algorithm's. Only mockup where the killer feature ("AI Cross-Platform Curator") is phrased as *"reads taste like a magazine editor reads readers"* — that sentence is demo-narration-ready.

### 🥈 #2 · the-designer (34 pts)
**Why it places**: Strongest pure visual craft (132pt mixed-blend headline, halftone numeral, rouge/pop/teal triad, "Bubble jailbreak ed." stamp). Demo first-frame is unmistakably *designed*, not generated — exactly the Demo 20% wedge in a sea of LLM-stamped UIs. Loses #1 only on accessibility (oversized type + mixed-blend-mode) and IA (hero buries the 9 picks). Companion to #1 — they are the same editorial DNA at different volume settings.

### 🥉 #3 · the-mobile-first (30 pts, tie-break over D)
**Why it places** (tie-break vs the-anti-ai): Both scored 30; the-mobile-first wins on **demo physicality** — judges can literally install ShortFlix on their phone during review (PWA install in 3 taps). The 9:16 player + cross-platform "via Instagram Reels" badge + in-card "why" chip is the most legible cross-platform-curator UI in the set. Anchors the locked PWA + hybrid-sync surface as a *literal product*, not a deck.

## 4. Dissent Log

- **UP08 Brand Voice + UP06 Content Designer**: Strong dissent for **the-anti-ai** at #1 — argue that "make the model invisible" is the most defensible 2026 UX position, and the literary blurbs ARE the discovery delight. Chair acknowledges: D's copy is the strongest in the set. Counter: panel-wide concern that judges *want to see* the multi-agent system (challenge rule: multi-agent superiority must be demonstrably stronger), and D hides it intentionally. Logged as minority-strong-preference.
- **UP10 Motion Designer**: Dissent against #1 (B is static-by-design). Argues that B + D both lack motion entry points for the 2-min demo's first 30s. Chair counters: B's *daily issue arrives* moment + dossier-export flow is sufficient motion. Logged.
- **UP05 Mobile UX**: Argues C should be #1 ∵ 87% mobile watch-time. Chair counters: B and D are responsive enough; C wins device-fit but loses demo-narrative ambition. Logged as known trade-off.
- **UP03 Accessibility flag (non-blocking)**: A and E need contrast/type-size remediation before ship. B and D are AA-clean as drawn.
